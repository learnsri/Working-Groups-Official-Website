Launch of “AI Literacy for All” at LEARN36

AI Literacy LEARN36 National Initiative Sinhala & Tamil

LEARN36 marked a major milestone in Sri Lanka’s digital education journey with the official launch of “AI Literacy for All” — a nationwide initiative expanding access to AI education in local languages.

The 36th Annual General Meeting of the Lanka Education And Research Network (LEARN36) marked a major milestone in Sri Lanka’s digital education journey with the official launch of “AI Literacy for All”, a nationwide initiative aimed at expanding access to artificial intelligence education in local languages.

LEARN36 brought together leaders from universities, government institutions, research organizations, and international partners to discuss digital transformation, AI literacy, and collaboration across Sri Lanka’s higher education and research sectors. The event highlighted LEARN’s evolution from a national research network into a platform for innovation, capacity building, and regional collaboration.

One of the most significant announcements at the AGM was the launch of the Sinhala and Tamil versions of the AI Literacy for All e-learning platform. Supported by AVPN Singapore, Google.org, and the Asian Development Bank, the initiative aims to train over 15,000 Sri Lankans, including 1,500 school teachers, 4,500 non-STEM university students, and 9,000 vocational and professional learners, ensuring inclusive access to foundational AI skills.

Originally developed in collaboration with UNESCO and the Asia Pacific University (APU), Malaysia, the platform has gained international recognition through the Asia Pacific Advanced Network (APAN). The localized versions address key challenges identified during early pilot programmes, particularly the need for AI learning materials in national languages and improved accessibility for under-resourced communities.

“The 15,000 target is only the first phase of a long-term vision to build an AI-ready nation.”

— Statement highlighted during the launch

Addressing the audience prior to the launch, Dr. Asitha Bandaranayake, Chief Technology Officer of LEARN, reflected on the journey that led to the initiative. He noted that workshops and pilot training programmes demonstrated both the demand for AI education and the importance of scaling these efforts nationally.

Senior Professor K. L. Wasantha Kumara, Chairman of LEARN, highlighted LEARN’s role as a non-profit enabler of digital education reform, stressing the importance of bridging the digital divide and integrating AI into teaching and research as a tool for empowerment. Delivering the Chief Guest’s Address, Hon. Eng. Eranga Weeraratne, Deputy Minister of Digital Economy, outlined national priorities including AI-ready digital infrastructure, local-language AI tools, inclusive capacity building, and stronger collaboration between academia, industry, and government.

The launch of AI Literacy for All, alongside the introduction of two new national working groups, Agriculture Technology (AgroTec) and Generative AI for Productivity (GenAI), reflects LEARN’s commitment to strengthening Sri Lanka’s digital ecosystem. LEARN36 reaffirmed the organisation’s role in connecting institutions, empowering learners, and advancing inclusive digital transformation across the country.
